TOL

Whitney, my love, You are in the wilderness in the middle of the night, alone. It is winter, and you’ve never seen so much undisturbed snow in your life. Not a branch, a paw, or foot has touched this part of the world it seems. The loneliness in the air is a kind you’ve never met before. You know how you can be sitting in an air conditioned room, and suddenly the air shuts off and you realize for the first time that it had made any noise at all? This is kind of like that, but instead of the sound of air, it’s the residue of life you are missing. Never had you been aware of the energy left behind to a place where there had been laughter, until you were in a place where there had never been any laughter at all. You don’t know why you’re here, when you came to be here, or even where here is, but you know you need to leave.
